Process Design Pitfalls That Could Be Costing You

By Ana Avendaño • 5 min read • September 21, 2024

Learn how to identify and avoid the most common process design mistakes that can drain your resources and hurt your bottom line.

The Hidden Costs of Poor Process Design

When businesses rush to implement new processes without proper planning, they often create more problems than they solve. These "quick fixes" can lead to bottlenecks, confusion, and wasted resources that compound over time.

Common Pitfall #1: Skipping the Discovery Phase

Many organizations jump straight into solution mode without understanding their current state. This leads to:

  • Automating broken processes
  • Missing critical stakeholder requirements
  • Creating solutions that don't address root causes

Common Pitfall #2: Over-Engineering Solutions

Complexity for the sake of complexity rarely pays off. Simple, well-designed processes often outperform elaborate systems that are difficult to maintain and understand.

Common Pitfall #3: Ignoring Change Management

The best process design in the world won't succeed if people don't adopt it. Consider:

  • Training requirements
  • Communication strategies
  • Gradual rollout plans

How to Avoid These Pitfalls

Successful process design requires a methodical approach that balances efficiency with practicality. Start with thorough discovery, design for your users, and plan for change management from day one.
